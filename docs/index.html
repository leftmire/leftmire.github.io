About Me

I am an early-career health data scientist from Hanover, New Hampshire. I graduated from Middelbury College with a B.A. in Computer Science and a minor in mathematics.

 

Despite entering college with an interest in healthcare, I quickly developed an affinity for the quantitative sciences. I could get lost in introductory coding problem sets for hours without boredom, and built my course schedule each semester around the coolest statistics classes.

 

However, I remained curious about healthcare. I dove into this interest by volunteering in hospitals, gaining an EMT license, and applying my senior coursework to healthcare applications of statistics and computer science. As I learned more about the utility of the quantitative sciences in healthcare, I began to envision an exciting career. I have worked in data science with small hospitals and large healthcare corporations, and I currently work as an Associate Data Scientist with N1 Health in Boston. 

 

In my free time, I enjoy various outdoor activities—running, hiking, biking, and backpacking. I also enjoy crafting; I have been dabbling in embroidery and have successfully knit a passable tank top and a mediocre sweater vest. I am currently working on a sweater! 

 

Please don't hesitate to reach out with anything data science, health tech, outdoors, or crafting related.
